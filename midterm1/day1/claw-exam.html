Fill in each cell [1 pt each]
Error SourceDay 1 ‚Üí Day 2 Change
Chance error
Selection bias
Non-response bias
Response bias
Show Solution

Chance error: ‚Üì Decreases

Sample size increased from 120 to 400. Larger samples ‚Üí less random variability.

Selection bias: ‚Üë Increases

Day 1 sampled from physical travelers at the curb (at least physically at the airport). Day 2 samples from app users only ‚Äî this is a biased frame that excludes non-tech-savvy travelers, elderly, international visitors without the app, etc. The sampling frame narrowed and became less representative.

Non-response bias: ‚Üì Decreases

Day 1 intercepts people rushing through an airport at 6‚Äì8 AM ‚Äî many will refuse. Email surveys, while they have their own non-response issues, allow people to respond at their convenience. The Day 2 approach also includes a reminder mechanism. Overall non-response is likely lower.

Response bias relates to whether respondents answer truthfully. Switching the survey delivery method doesn't inherently change whether people lie or misremember their rideshare habits.

ü™§ Trap: Students often confuse "bigger sample = less bias." Increasing sample size reduces CHANCE ERROR only. It does nothing for systematic biases (selection, response). Here, the Day 2 method actually INCREASES selection bias despite having more respondents.
